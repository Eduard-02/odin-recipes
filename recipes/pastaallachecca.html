Pasta Alla Checca

Description

During the summer season, when succulent tomatoes are at the pinnacle of their ripeness, Rome locals
have a particular affinity for a pasta dish renowned for its simple, fresh ingredients and uncompromised
flavors. Add to this, that Pasta alla Checca is the easiest pasta dish on the planet to make. No joke!

Ingredients

  • 300 grams of Fusilli Pasta Liguori
  • 500 grams of Cherry tomatoes
  • 2 Buffalo mozzarella
  • Pecorino cheese as much as preferred
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • Basil leaves as much as preferred
  • Extra virgin olive oil EVOO
  • Salt & pepper as much as required

Steps

  1. Cut cherry tomatoes in half and add them to a large bowl.

  2. Gently squeeze the tomatoes with a potato masher to get the juice out of them.

  3. Break basil leaves into small pieces and add them to the bowl.

  4. Add a generous amount of salt and pepper to the bowl and give everything a nice stir.

  5. Add a tablespoon of rock salt or sea salt to a pot of boiling water and boil the pasta
    according to package instructions. Make sure to give it a quick stir to prevent the pasta
    from sticking to the bottom of the pan.

  6. Strain the pasta, let it cool, and drizzle some EVOO to prevent it from sticking together.

  7. Cut buffalo mozzarella and pecorino cheese into bite-sized cubes, add half of them to the
    tomato mixture, and mix everything until the tomatoes, the juices, and the cheese meld together.

  8. Add pasta to the bowl and quickly mix everything to let the pasta absorb all the juices.

  9. Add the rest of the cheeses with a finishing touch of basil. Serve and Enjoy!
